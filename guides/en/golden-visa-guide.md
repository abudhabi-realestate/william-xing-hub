# Golden Visa — Frequently Asked Questions

> Compiled from UAE federal residency policy and property investment practice.  
> Policies may change; thresholds and documentation are subject to the latest requirements from ICA / GDRFA and developers. This does not constitute legal or investment advice.

---

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: What is the difference between a Golden Visa and a standard employment visa?**

A standard employment visa is tied to an employer; resignation or company changes may affect your residency status. The Golden Visa is a **long-term residency status**, commonly valid for **10 years** and renewable, **without employer sponsorship**. It suits families seeking lawful residency through investment, talent, entrepreneurship, or other qualifying routes.

**Q2: Can I obtain a Golden Visa by buying property?**

Yes. Property investment is **one of the main routes** to a Golden Visa: holding qualifying real estate in the UAE allows you to apply for long-term residency for yourself and eligible dependants.

**Q3: What is the minimum property value for a Golden Visa?**

The current common threshold is property valued at **≥ 2 million AED** (approximately CNY 3.8 million+, subject to exchange rate fluctuations). This applies to **freehold** property within Investment Zones in emirates such as Dubai and Abu Dhabi.

**Q4: Are the thresholds the same in Abu Dhabi and Dubai?**

**No — the payment milestone differs:**

- **Dubai:** You may apply once the **down payment** is paid (a common path for mortgaged property);
- **Abu Dhabi:** You must have paid a cumulative total of at least **2 million AED** before applying.

Both cities require freehold title, but approving authorities, document checklists, and processing times may differ slightly. Confirm with the local GDRFA / ICA service centre.

**Q5: Can I use off-plan property, or must it be completed?**

**Requirements are the same in Dubai and Abu Dhabi:** Both off-plan and completed property qualify. What matters is whether payment progress meets each emirate’s threshold (down payment paid in Dubai / cumulative 2 million AED in Abu Dhabi) and whether title and developer documentation are complete.

**Q6: Can I apply with a mortgage?**

Yes. For mortgaged property, you must first obtain a **NOC** (No Objection Certificate) from the **mortgage lender**. Visa processing can proceed only after the bank approves. Confirm NOC conditions and validity with your lender before taking the loan.

**Q7: Can I combine multiple properties to reach 2 million AED?**

**Yes.** Multiple properties may be aggregated to meet the threshold. They may be in different projects, but all must be within freehold areas, with clear title and payment records.

**Q8: Can family members apply together?**

**Yes.** Eligible **immediate family members** may obtain dependant residency, including **spouse, children, and parents** (age and other details per policy at the time of application).

**Q9: Is there a minimum number of days I must stay in the UAE each year?**

Investment-category Golden Visas **generally have no mandatory physical presence requirement** (unlike some countries’ permanent residency programmes) — a key draw for many investors. Note, however:

- **Tax residency (TRC)** follows separate day-count rules, unrelated to visa status;
- If you are absent from the UAE long term, you still need local arrangements for property rental, banking, insurance, and related matters.

**Q10: What happens after 10 years?**

You may apply for **renewal**, typically subject to continued property holding requirements, no serious legal violations, and complete documentation. If the property has been sold, confirm whether renewal eligibility is affected.

**Q11: Can I sell the property immediately after obtaining the visa?**

**No.** The Golden Visa is linked to **ongoing property ownership**. Selling during the holding period may prevent renewal or invalidate the visa. Treat the visa as part of a **medium- to long-term holding plan**, not a “buy, get the visa, then flip” arbitrage tool.

**Q12: Must I live in the property used for the visa?**

**No.** You may rent it out, but leases must be registered in compliance with local rules (e.g. Ejari in Dubai / Tawtheeq in Abu Dhabi), and service charges and related fees must be paid. Rental generally does not affect the visa as long as **title remains and thresholds are met**, though extended vacancy may affect some banks’ or authorities’ assessment.

**Q13: Where do buyers commonly look to meet the 2 million AED threshold?**

Dubai offers broad choice and high liquidity; Abu Dhabi also accepts Investment Zone property, with **two-bedroom units in some communities landing near the 2 million AED band** — balancing self-use, rental, and visa goals (see the Abu Dhabi 30-Day location analysis series). Evaluate **title type, transfer clarity, and liquidity**, not threshold alone.

**Q14: Does a Golden Visa make me a UAE tax resident?**

**No.** The visa addresses **lawful residency**; tax residency depends on **actual days present and centre of life**, and requires a separate TRC application to the FTA. A Golden Visa does **not automatically confer** tax residency.

**Q15: Does a Golden Visa automatically end my China tax residency?**

**No.** Whether you remain a China tax resident depends on domicile, days of presence, and other tests under PRC tax law. Buying property abroad and holding a visa **does not automatically** change your China tax status; cross-border compliance requires separate planning.

**Q16: Can a Golden Visa help me avoid CRS reporting?**

**No.** If you remain a China tax resident, offshore financial accounts may still be exchanged under CRS. Holding a long-term visa of 5 years or more (including a Golden Visa) may instead trigger **enhanced due diligence (EDD)** from banks.

**Q17: Is “buy property, immigrate” accurate?**

A more accurate description is **“buy property + meet conditions → long-term residency visa.”** The UAE Golden Visa is **not a passport** and does not involve nationality change; it is a tool for lawful long-term residency and family settlement.
